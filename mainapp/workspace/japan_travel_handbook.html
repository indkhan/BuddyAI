    A beautiful stone path alongside a canal, lined with hundreds of cherry trees (though not in bloom in June, it's still serene). At dusk, it offers a quiet, romantic atmosphere. Find a secluded spot along the path for an intimate proposal.

  • Fushimi Inari Shrine (Early Morning/Late Evening)

    Famous for its thousands of vibrant vermilion torii gates winding up a sacred mountain. To avoid crowds and create a magical, intimate setting, visit at dawn or late in the evening. The ethereal glow of the lanterns and the quiet atmosphere can be truly breathtaking.

  • Heian Jingu Shrine Garden

    While the shrine itself is grand, the surrounding gardens (paid entry) are expansive and beautifully designed, offering many tranquil spots. It's a serene environment for a heartfelt proposal amidst traditional Japanese landscaping.

Tip: Whichever location you choose, consider the time of day to maximize privacy and the magical ambiance. Early mornings or late evenings are often best for popular spots.

Travel Tips for Your Journey

Transportation

  • Japan Rail Pass: For 7 days, a JR Pass might not be cost-effective if you're only doing Tokyo-Kyoto-Nara. Consider individual Shinkansen tickets or a regional pass if available. Use a JR Pass calculator online to check.
  • IC Cards (Suica/Pasmo/Icoca): Get one upon arrival for easy travel on local trains and subways in major cities. You can also use them at convenience stores and vending machines.
  • Walking: Japan's cities are very walkable. Wear comfortable shoes!
  • Google Maps: Essential for navigating public transport and walking directions.

Money & Budget

  • Cash is King: While cards are accepted in many places, cash is still widely used, especially at smaller shops, restaurants, and temples. Carry enough yen.
  • ATMs: Look for ATMs at 7-Eleven convenience stores or Japan Post Offices for international card withdrawals.
  • Budgeting: Your budget of $2500-5000 for 2 people for 7 days (excluding flights) is manageable. This allows for mid-range accommodation, good food, and entry fees. Eating at convenience stores (konbini), local ramen shops, or udon/soba places can save money.

Culture & Etiquette

  • Bow: A slight bow is a common greeting and sign of respect.
  • Shoes: Remove your shoes when entering homes, traditional restaurants, temples, and some hotels. Look for shoe lockers or designated areas.
  • Chopsticks: Don't stick chopsticks upright in your rice (it resembles a funeral ritual) or pass food directly from chopstick to chopstick.
  • Tipping: Tipping is not customary in Japan and can sometimes be considered rude.
  • Noise: Be mindful of noise levels, especially on public transport. Avoid loud conversations or phone calls.
  • Rubbish Bins: Public rubbish bins are scarce. Carry a small bag for your trash until you find one.

Connectivity

  • Pocket Wi-Fi or SIM Card: Highly recommended for staying connected and using maps. Rent a pocket Wi-Fi device at the airport or buy a data-only SIM card.

June Weather

  • June marks the start of the rainy season (tsuyu) in much of Japan. Expect humidity and frequent rain showers.
  • Pack light, breathable clothing, a compact umbrella, and waterproof shoes.
  • Temperatures will be warm, ranging from 20-28°C (68-82°F).
