Japan Travel Handbook: Kyoto & Nara

Welcome to your personalized travel handbook for your unforgettable 7-day journey through the cultural heart of Japan! This guide is designed to help you navigate Kyoto and Nara, discover hidden gems, immerse yourselves in Japanese culture, and find that perfect moment for your special proposal.

Trip Dates: June 1 - 8

Focus: Historical sites, hidden gems, Japanese culture (kendo, tea ceremonies, Zen meditation), Nara deer, walking tours, and a special proposal location.

7-Day Itinerary Overview

Day 1: Sunday, June 2 - Arrival in Kyoto & Gion Exploration

  • Arrive at KIX, transfer to Kyoto.
  • Check into accommodation.
  • Explore Gion, Kyoto's geisha district.
  • Hidden Gem: Wander through Pontocho Alley.

Day 2: Monday, June 3 - Golden Pavilions & Zen Gardens

  • Visit Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion).
  • Contemplate at Ryoan-ji Temple's rock garden.
  • Experience a traditional Tea Ceremony.
  • Explore Nishiki Market (Kyoto's Kitchen).

Day 3: Tuesday, June 4 - Thousand Gates & Bamboo Groves

  • Hike through Fushimi Inari-taisha Shrine's torii gates.
  • Explore Arashiyama: Bamboo Grove, Tenryu-ji Temple, Togetsukyo Bridge.

Day 4: Wednesday, June 5 - Nara Deer & Proposal Day!

  • Day trip to Nara: Nara Park, Todai-ji Temple, Kasuga Taisha Shrine.
  • Proposal Location: Nara Park or Kasuga Taisha Shrine.
Remember this special day! Nara offers unique and beautiful spots for your proposal. Consider a quiet area in Nara Park or within the serene grounds of Kasuga Taisha Shrine.

Day 5: Thursday, June 6 - Cultural Immersion & Hidden Temples

  • Participate in a Zen Meditation session.
  • Visit Kiyomizu-dera Temple and Sannenzaka/Ninenzaka slopes.
  • Seek out a Kendo experience/demonstration.
  • Hidden Gem: Explore Kodai-ji Temple.

Day 6: Friday, June 7 - Kyoto's Charm & Departure Prep

  • Visit Nijo Castle.
  • Stroll along the Philosopher's Path and visit Ginkaku-ji (Silver Pavilion).
  • Last-minute souvenir shopping.

Day 7: Saturday, June 8 - Departure from Japan

  • Enjoy a final Japanese breakfast.
  • Transfer from Kyoto to Kansai International Airport (KIX) for your flight home.

Attraction Descriptions & Maps

Kyoto

  • Gion: Kyoto's most famous geisha district. Explore traditional wooden machiya houses and Hanamikoji Street. View on Google Maps
  • Pontocho Alley: A narrow, atmospheric alleyway running parallel to the Kamo River, filled with restaurants and bars. View on Google Maps
  • Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion): A stunning Zen temple covered in gold leaf, set against a beautiful pond. View on Google Maps
  • Ryoan-ji Temple: Famous for its minimalist rock garden, a perfect spot for quiet contemplation. View on Google Maps
  • Nishiki Market: Known as "Kyoto's Kitchen," a vibrant market street offering all kinds of food, from fresh seafood to traditional sweets. View on Google Maps
  • Fushimi Inari-taisha Shrine: Famous for its thousands of vibrant orange torii gates winding up a sacred mountain. View on Google Maps
  • Arashiyama Bamboo Grove: An iconic and magical bamboo forest. View on Google Maps
  • Tenryu-ji Temple: A UNESCO World Heritage site in Arashiyama with beautiful gardens. View on Google Maps
  • Kiyomizu-dera Temple: A UNESCO World Heritage site with a large wooden stage offering panoramic views of Kyoto. View on Google Maps
  • Kodai-ji Temple: A beautiful temple with stunning gardens and a bamboo grove, often less crowded than other major sites. View on Google Maps
  • Nijo Castle: A UNESCO World Heritage site known for its "nightingale floors" and beautiful gardens. View on Google Maps
  • Philosopher's Path (Tetsugaku no Michi): A pleasant stone path alongside a canal, ideal for a leisurely stroll. View on Google Maps
  • Ginkaku-ji (Silver Pavilion): A Zen temple with a unique sand garden and beautiful grounds. View on Google Maps

Nara

  • Nara Park: Home to thousands of friendly wild deer. View on Google Maps
  • Todai-ji Temple: Houses a massive bronze Buddha statue and an impressive wooden gate. View on Google Maps
  • Kasuga Taisha Shrine: Known for its hundreds of bronze and stone lanterns. View on Google Maps

Travel Tips for Japan

Transportation

  • ICOCA/SUICA Card: These rechargeable IC cards are incredibly convenient for trains, subways, and buses in major cities. You can purchase them at major train stations.
  • Google Maps: Essential for navigating public transport. It provides accurate train and bus schedules, platform numbers, and even walking directions.
  • Walking: Japan's cities are very walkable. Wear comfortable shoes!
  • Trains: Punctual and efficient. Learn to read station signs and train lines.

Etiquette & Culture

  • Bowing: A common form of greeting and showing respect. A slight nod is usually sufficient for tourists.
  • Shoes: Remove your shoes when entering homes, traditional restaurants, temples, and some hotels. Look for shoe lockers or designated areas.
  • Chopsticks: Do not stick chopsticks upright in your rice (resembles funeral rites) or pass food directly from chopstick to chopstick.
  • Noise: Keep noise levels down on public transport and in quiet areas like temples.
  • Tipping: Tipping is not customary in Japan and can sometimes be considered rude.

Money & Shopping

  • Cash: While credit cards are accepted in many places, cash is still king, especially at smaller shops, restaurants, and vending machines. Carry enough yen.
  • ATMs: Look for ATMs at 7-Eleven convenience stores or post offices, which are generally reliable for international card withdrawals.
  • Tax-Free Shopping: Many larger stores offer tax-free shopping for tourists on purchases over a certain amount. Look for "Tax-Free" signs.

General Tips

  • Wi-Fi: Rent a pocket Wi-Fi device or purchase a local SIM card for reliable internet access. Free Wi-Fi is available in some public areas and hotels.
  • Convenience Stores (Konbini): 7-Eleven, FamilyMart, and Lawson are lifesavers for snacks, drinks, quick meals, and ATMs.
  • Rubbish Bins: Public rubbish bins are scarce. Carry a small bag for your trash until you find one.
  • June Weather: June is the start of the rainy season in Japan. Be prepared for humidity and occasional rain. Pack light, breathable clothing and a compact umbrella.
  • Enjoy the Food: Be adventurous! Japanese cuisine is diverse and delicious. Don't be afraid to try local specialties.
